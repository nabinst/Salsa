Our Story

Nepal to U.S.

  • 2007 - 2017

    Our Hope

    Everest Salsa is owned and operated by Nabin Shrestha, a Software Engineer who loves to cook for his family and friends. He lives with his wife, Kerri, and their two boys, Kade and Everest. The title for our salsa was inspired from our first-born’s name, Everest McKinley Shrestha. His name comes from the highest peak in Nepal and in the U.S.A. It represents our desire to integrate the culture and values of both countries into our children’s lives. Food is one of the most authentic ways to experience another culture. It is our hope that you will experience a small taste of Nepal through Everest Salsa.

  • May 2015

    The business concept was Born

    The story of Everest Salsa begins in 2007 with our family’s return to the United States. Most of our loved ones were excited to try Nepali food. The aroma of Basmati rice, the spicy vegetables, and chicken curry were a big hit, but the salsa was something special. Friends began asking if we could bring it as a dip for chips or veggies. Some put it on their burgers, while others mixed it with casseroles and other dishes. At our house, we like it with eggs in the morning and chips in the evening. It tastes good with just about anything.

  • January 2017

    Everest Salsa: "chutne" with an American twist.

    Chutne is a side used in Nepal with most meals. Everest Salsa is a blend of Nepali chutne and our own ideas about what an American might crave in a salsa. Experience the heat of a cilantro and tomoto based salsa that is based off of one of our favorite Nepali foods.

  • November 2017

    Hobby to a business

    At this point, Everest Salsa is still a hobby, but we hope that it will become something more for us. If you like Everest Salsa, spread the word.

FAQ's

Who do I call if I have problem ordering through the web?

If you have any difficulties with placing your order, please email at everestsalsa@gmail.com or call at 4233102097.

Can I freeze the Everest Salsa?

We do not advise freezing our product. It’s fresh and best consumed within a week or two.

How long does it take to deliver the salsa once an order is placed?

Currently, by-weekly, I make a batch usually (Friday or Saturday) and It can be picked up from my house or I can arrange to deliver based on the situation.

I like the taste, but it’s a bit too hot for me. Can you make a mild version?

Yes. If you order at least 5 jars or more.
